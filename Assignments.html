Week by Week

Failures, successes, and everything in between.

Week 2 HTML

We'll start with week two because week one consisted of reading the syllabus and course expectations. I'll be honest, I was scared to take this class. My faith in my ability to learn this material so quickly was about a 2 out of 10. Week 2, we dove right in to HTML and our whirlwind adventure was underway.
HTML was pretty straightforward for me. The codecamp exercises were pretty easy and I started feeling like maybe this is how the rest of the course would be, relatively easy.
In this first assignment, we were asked to build three pages called nested, attributes, and tags to show how to perform each of those functions with a variety of elements in HTML.
The part of the assignment that was the most challenging was creating a nav bar. I ended up looking at the html used on another web page to figure out how to create my first ever navigation from scratch.

Week 3 CSS

A week went by and we were on to the next thing! Week three we wrote our CSS files and learned how to reference them within our html pages. I focused on the assignment requirements and aimed to change 10 style selectors within my referenced CSS. I did this assignment mostly on this page and also changed some of the styles on one of my previously built Attributes page.
My struggles during this assignment were all related to the page layout. I couldn't figure out how to make columns for my lists! Good thing bootstrap cam along because I was feeling pretty defeated with my bland styling examples.

Week 4 JavaScript

Silly me, I thought JavaScript would be easy! I write it into my PDF forms with Adobe, I hide and show fields based on selections, I edit text, I've done this before! This will be a snap!
Boy, was I wrong. I will say that the JavaScript assignment is the one I struggled with the most. I still haven't got it to work the way I wanted! I felt like I was banging my head on the desk for the whole week with nothing to show for it. Once again, I struggled with layout, but this time I also struggled with my script working locally but not on GitHub. Dr. Dan, our professor, give me some pointers on my assignment that I may have missed a label somewhere, but I never found it.
Now, I'm a pretty emotional person. When I turned my assignment in, I felt completely hopelessly lost. I was wondering how in the world I was going to understand Bootstrap when I couldn't even get these JavaScript functions to work?
Fast forward to today, and I'm feeling pretty good about the progress I made since this, "low point" and I'm so glad I didn't give up.

Week 5 By Week Start Final

After the JavaScript debacle, I needed to catch my breath. Week 5 was perfectly timed. We watched videos and read articles on bootstrap, but no actual bootstrap development was expected. This week we started work on our final project pages. I went back to my html and CSS roots and ended up with a page I enjoyed for my final landing page. I also created what would eventually be my Team and About me pages.
I was still fearing Bootstrap, even after the assigned readings and videos, but I felt rested and ready to take it on.

Week 6 Bootstrap!

To say I caught on quickly would be an understatement in my opinion. Who are these guys who worked for Twitter and as a side project developed this wonderful new way to develop for the web!? I could kiss them, I really could.
While developing the bootstrap page, I relied mostly on W3 Schools and the Getting Started with Bootstrap site posted to our reading assignments for that week. These sites had information on all the requested elements.
Now that I have built a few pages using Bootstrap, I'm never going back. What a perfectly wonderful, helpful development tool.

Week 7 Final Project

When I said I wanted to kiss those Bootstrap fellas, I meant it. The one thing I struggled with the most throughout the course was my page layouts. Bootstrap makes it so much easier to understand how to lay out your page. I defiantly want to revisit portions of this course so I can feel more confident in the different languages we've used. However, if I compare my knowledge in web development now to that of seven weeks ago, I have achieved a great deal. It feels really good to have tackled something so initially overwhelming to me. I will never regret the time I spent, even the frustrations I faced along the way because this moment feels SO good.
Now maybe I can take that vacation I need so badly, or at least a staycation. All while feeling completely confident and at ease in my achievements during this nutty time.
